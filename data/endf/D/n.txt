METHODOLOGY USED IN NEW EVALUATION                                 128 1451   75
                                                                   128 1451   76
  The neutron total cross section is based on ENDF/B-VI below      128 1451   77
100 MeV and on experimental data between 100 and 200 MeV.  The     128 1451   78
elastic scattering, nonelastic, and 2H(n,2n)1H cross sections      128 1451   79
were determined above 10 MeV in concert, using the fact that the   128 1451   80
nonelastic cross section essentially equals the (n,2n) cross       128 1451   81
section above En ~ 1 keV, and the elastic and nonelastic cross     128 1451   82
sections must sum to the total cross section.  Note that we        128 1451   83
revised the existing ENDF/B-VI (n,2n) cross section at most        128 1451   84
energies between 10 and 100 MeV to improve the agreement with      128 1451   85
experimental data.  We also included experimental data for the     128 1451   86
proton reaction cross section of 2H in our analysis, as we         128 1451   87
determined empirically that it is entirely consistent with the     128 1451   88
neutron nonelastic (or reaction) cross section above an incident   128 1451   89
nucleon energy of about 20 MeV.                                    128 1451   90
                                                                   128 1451   91
  The elastic cross section and angular distributions below an     128 1451   92
incident energy of 4 MeV were validated by comparing to results    128 1451   93
of a coupled-channels R-matrix analysis.  No changes were made     128 1451   94
to the existing ENDF/B-VI.2 cross section evaluation because the   128 1451   95
results were consistent with data and the R-matrix analysis;       128 1451   96
the elastic angular distributions below 3.2 MeV were replaced      128 1451   97
with tabulated distributions from the R-matrix analysis.           128 1451   98
                                                                   128 1451   99
  Several elastic scattering angular distribution measurements     128 1451  100
exist above 20 MeV which we fit with Legendre expansions to get    128 1451  101
integrated cross sections and to establish the evaluated angular   128 1451  102
distributions.  Especially important are the measurements of       128 1451  103
Romero, Wang, and Howard, as well as the partial distributions     128 1451  104
of Yountz and Palmieri.  We inferred nonelastic cross sections     128 1451  105
from the elastic scattering angular distribution measurements      128 1451  106
above 20 MeV, combined with our evaluated total cross section      128 1451  107
from experimental data.                                            128 1451  108
                                                                   128 1451  109
  We assume that the energy distributions of neutrons from         128 1451  110
2H(n,2n)1H follow a pure three-body phase space distribution at    128 1451  111
all incident neutron energies and utilize LAW=6 in MF=6 to         128 1451  112
represent these energy distributions.                              128 1451  113
